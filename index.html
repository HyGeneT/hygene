Dream

As a kid, I dreamed of castles and fighting dragons
Of sailing the seas and firing cannons,
Clashing swords and defeating monsters,
And exploring the stars and its strange encounters

But as I grew up, life yielded to the world's pressure
Questions began to pile up without an answer
Reality took the place of imagination and laughter
The stars I dreamed about now seemed less brighter

I came to realize things are no longer as they were
Those dreams have become but a fond memoir
An obsolete possession that time wanted me to let go
But I'm thankful I didn't because now I know

That the castle could be built brick by brick
And the journey to the stars need not be quick
The dragons I fight constantly change their form
And even without a ship, I can get through a raging storm

I don't have to stop dreaming of something that will not be
But still recognize even in the small things there is beauty
And when I think I cannot be more than what I've thought
I remember I was once a warrior, a prince and even an astronaut


Divide

Anguished souls lost in a world of divide
No safe space for a pained heart to confide
When even our own empathy, we decide
We've deemed a weakness that we choose to hide

Yet your own humanity would have decried:
"Why can't we choose to walk alongside?
We pick those to keep and those to throw aside
And yet we'd have our own conscience satisfied

We know not of their stories, what they keep inside
Nor what they've gone through or what they've tried
Then, who are we to say they're unqualified?
Is it not our own flaws that keep us unified?

At the end of the day, aren't our fates all tied?"
